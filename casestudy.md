# Case Study: Phishing Attack on a College Student

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers pretend to be someone trustworthy (like a bank, college, or government) to trick people into giving away their personal information such as passwords, OTP, or bank details.

How it typically happens (step-by-step):
1. The student receives an email or WhatsApp message that looks official (e.g., “Your VIT library account is suspended – click here to verify”).
2. The link takes them to a fake website that looks exactly like the real VIT portal or bank site.
3. They enter their username, password, and OTP.
4. The attacker immediately gets full access to their account.

Who is usually targeted?
College students in India are prime targets because they use UPI apps, online shopping, and college portals frequently and often click links quickly.

Consequences:
- Money can be stolen from bank accounts.
- Personal data (photos, marksheets, Aadhaar) can be misused.
- The student may face identity theft or blackmail.

In one real case in Madhya Pradesh, a student lost ₹45,000 after clicking a fake “VIT fee payment” link. This shows how dangerous phishing is even for smart students.
